PC Gaming

Hello and welcome to my dedicated hobby webpage. In this section I'll share with you my story, the spark that ignited my love for PC Gaming, and why I believe you might love it too.

It was the year 1999 was when I first dove into the world of PC gaming with a strategy game called Pharaoh. I'd already played video games on a Sega Genesis before, but the world of PC gaming was a revelation, opening up new posibilities of interaction and engagement.

After Pharaoh, I dove headlong into The Sims, Half-Life, and the then popular mod for Half-Life, Counter-Strike Version 1.0. My gaming journey has since spanned decades, featuring hundreds, possibly thousands, of gaming titles. Fast forward to the present, my current most played titles are CS:GO 2.0, Overwatch 2, Valorant, and Chivalry 2.

Contrasting console gaming, PC gaming gives the user freedom that is both limitless and dangerous. This leaves lots of room for error, but it's perfect for those of us with an innate desire to probe, tinker, and occasionally break things. This intriguing blend of freedom and complexity might not appeal to everyone, but if you're someone who thrives on exploration and challenges, then PC gaming could be for you.

Streaming

Streaming on Twitch is a unique digital experience that combines interactive entertainment, community building, and live broadcasting. Essentially, it's about sharing live video content, typically gameplay, with viewers from around the world. As a Twitch streamer, you're not just playing games; you're also interacting with your audience in real-time, responding to chat, taking suggestions, and even sharing aspects of your everyday life.

Streaming is also not just about gaming. Twitch is a platform where streamers can broadcast a variety of content, including creative arts, music performances, coding sessions, or simply just chatting with viewers. While the platform was originally designed for gamers, it has since expanded to a broader audience, with channels dedicated to a variety of hobbies and interests.

What sets Twitch apart is the sense of community it fosters. It's not just a one-way street; viewers interact with streamers and each other through live chat, creating a vibrant, dynamic environment. The more you stream, the more you engage, the stronger this community grows. Streaming on Twitch is an interactive, engaging, and communal experience. Whether you're a streamer or a viewer, there's always something new to discover, learn, and share.

PC Requirements for Gaming

If you're thinking about diving into the world of PC gaming, you'll need to consider your hardware. PC requirements can vary greatly based on the type of games you're interested in, but here's a basic rundown to get you started.

At the heart of your PC is the processor or CPU. For casual gaming, something like an modern Intel i5 or AMD Ryzen 5 should do the trick. If you're leaning towards more graphic-intensive games or plan on multitasking with streaming or video editing, you might want to look at an i7 or Ryzen 7.

Your graphics card or GPU is crucial for gaming. A mid-range GPU like NVIDIA GTX 1660 or AMD Radeon RX 6600 is a good starting point. If you're aiming for high-end gaming with maxed out settings or VR, consider a GPU like the NVIDIA RTX 4070 or an AMD RX 6800 or higher.

RAM is your PC's short-term memory. 8GB of RAM is the absolute minimum for gaming these days, but I'd recommend at least 16GB for a smooth experience.

A Solid State Drive (SSD) is a must-have for faster loading times. A 1TB SSD should be sufficient, but if you plan on downloading a lot of games, consider a 2TB+ SSD or adding a secondary SSD for extra storage. Stay away from old spinning Hard-Drives.

Don't forget about a good monitor. Look for something with a refresh rate of at least 75Hz (165Hz+ if you play a lot of fast-paced games like Valorant) and a resolution that matches your GPU's capabilities.

Lastly, you'll need a decent keyboard and mouse. It doesn't have to be anything flashy, just comfortable and responsive. I would recommend a simple mechanical keyboard and a gaming mouse with very low latency and a good sensor.

Remember, PC gaming is about gradual upgrades. You don't need the best rig right off the bat. Start with what suits your needs and budget, then upgrade components over time as needed.

Gear Required for Streaming

If you're thinking about the gear you need to get started with streaming, you're in the right place. For your base setup, refer to the PC gaming hardware guide I shared earlier - the same principles apply. But streaming needs a few extras.

The star of your stream is going to be a solid microphone. Your viewers need to hear you clearly. It doesn't need to break the bank, but definitely don't skimp here. A USB condenser mic is a good starting point, and you'll want a pop filter to catch those pesky "p" and "b" sounds.

Next up, consider a webcam. It adds a personal touch and viewers generally like to see the person behind the voice. A 720p webcam is workable, but a 1080p one offers a clearer image and isn't much more expensive.

Lighting is often overlooked but can make a big difference. Good lighting will make your webcam feed look much better. Simple desk lamps can work, but adjustable LED panels or a ring light offer more control.

Don't forget about your internet connection. Streaming requires a solid upload speed, aim for at least 35 Mbps for smooth 1080p streaming. I recommend just going with Gigabit, with this out of the way you won't have to worry about your internet again.

Lastly, invest in comfortable and durable peripherals. Streaming sessions can be long, so a good keyboard, mouse, and chair can make a world of difference.

Remember, you don't need to have all the top gear from day one. Start with what you can, then upgrade as you go.
